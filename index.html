Spare Change

Partners: Hector Bustillos, Christopher Wong, Phoebe Lim, Linda Shu
Programs Used: DAQRI 4D Studio Augmented Reality Program, Adobe Illustrator
My Role: Concept Ideation, Interviewer, Media Content Creator

Objective

This final project of our interactive art course was to integrate artistic and interactive strategies into one final art piece. Initially inspired by the “Humans of New York” blog, An idea of photographing students around campus and gathering their background stories was born.

Process

Our art piece was lacking in meaning as well as interaction. Walking past homeless individuals often, I suggest that we can interview them instead. I suggested homeless individuals because they’re so often neglected and not all of them ended up in that situation at their own action; I was curious about their life stories.

Process

The new design goal was to interview homeless individuals with deep questions that we all ponder as human beings in an interactive and innovative way. This interview would give the unhoused individuals a new dimension we so seldom see. Thus rehumanizing them through communication. With a new meaningful design goal in mind, we needed to implement an interactive element to our project.

Process

After group discussions, we agreed upon using augmented reality. We used DAQRI 4D software to put the each interview onto a poster. The poster itself was a grim back alley, and only when a user hover over the poster with the DAQRI app, would each interviewee appear on the screen, which the users can then click on to learn more about. It accurately reflected the ignored nature of these individuals, yet would literally give a new dimension to the interviewed.

Process

The group and I split into 2 groups to interview the homeless around Granville and Burrard skytrain stations. We asked questions about their dreams, fears, most treasured memories, favourite songs, plans for the future, and many other questions to round out their character in multi dimensional aspects.

Reflection

We are very pleased with the project. We’ve had more fun than we expected making a real human connection with these people. Our project reflected the status of the homeless in a personal matter and the interactive element added to the meaning of the project. However if given more time, we would have liked to include the audio / video recordings onto the poster as well, which we wanted to but unfortunately couldn’t due to a deadline restraint.

Heroes and Dragons

Partners: George Krutous, Kelvin Lam, Maggie Liu
Programs Used: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop
My Role: Lead Game Designer, Illustrator, Editor

Objective

Our final assignment was to construct and demonstrate a polished physical board game which uses a complex economic system. My team and I chose a medieval and magical setting for the board game, and we started brainstorming off the many games and game mechanics that we’ve experienced in our lives.

Process

We were given a very basic board game and was given the goal to redesign our game around it. Starting off with a variety of resources, we need to turn those resources into ways the players could win the game. Finally, we made the board into a symetrical and balanced board for resources and events.

Process

We took notice of multiple victory paths in Civilization and Settlers of Catan and took a similar approach: Players can earn victory points by building their city, or slaying the dragon. To achieve that, the players would need to choose to convert their resources into buildings for their city.

Process

My team and I easily came up with a wide array of ideas to implement into the game such as item crafting, resource trading, hero abilities, random event tiles, daily events, etc. That way players can choose their path by exploring events, managing resources, crafting items to upgrade their hero.

Process

When we conducted gameplay tests on our prototype, we realized that the game lasted too long. We lowered the victory point requirement, introduced a new gold resource which could replace any other resource as currency, and changed the map for faster movement with more events for more flow in gameplay & economy.

Reflection

Our final board game was revised with much higher fidelity. Since we were so excited to create our own game and tried to cater to all levels and types of players, we quickly ran out of time in our schedule and had to cut down on play testing just to finish the project.

Reflection

If I was given the chance to do this project again, I would focus down on a few game mechanics instead of making such a broad game that had everything. We easily satisfied the economy and complexity standards but lost marks for lateness since we needed an extra day to finish.

About Me

Hi There! My name is Ivan, I'm currently a fourth year SFU student majoring in interactive arts and technology with a curious mind and an interest in all things interactive.

I was introduced to games as a child and I soon fell in love with interactive mediums because unlike movies and books, my actions have consequences in both victory and defeat. This realization was empowering, especially when good times spent playing is with even good-er company!

My passion lies in seeing people smile, and I aim to do so through my interactive projects :)
